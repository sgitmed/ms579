Dark Patterns

Dark pattern design choices exploit user psychology to the benefit of the developer or company. These manipulative tricks coerce or decieve users to take actions they otherwise would not have. Deceptive design and dark patterns frequently find themselves in the digital marketplace, where users can be pushed to purchase a product, service, or subscription.

The list of dark patterns below and their subsequent examples are prodivded by deceptive.design

Types of Dark Patterns

Comparison Prevention

The user struggles to compare products because features and prices are combined in a complex manner, or because essential information is hard to find

Learn more

Confirmshaming

The user is emotionally manipulated into doing something that they otherwise would not have done.

Learn more

Disguised Ads

The user mistakenly believes they are clicking on an interface element or native content, but it's actually a disguised adversisement

Learn more

Fake Scarcity

The user is pressured into completing an action because they are presented with a fake indication of limited supply or popularity

Learn more

Fake Social Proof

The user is misled into believing a product is more popular or credible than it really is because they were shown fake reviews, testimonials, or activity messages.

Learn more

Hard to Cancel

The user finds it easy to sign up or subscribe, but when they want to cancel it is very hard to do so.

Learn more

Hidden Costs

The user is enticed with a low advertised price. After investing time and effort, they discover unexpected fees and charges when they reach the checkout.

Learn more

Sneaking

The user is drawn into a transaction on false pretences because pertinent information is hidden or delayed from being presented to them.
